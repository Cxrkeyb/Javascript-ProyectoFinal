M.R.U.

Uniform rectilinear motion (M.R.U.) describes the displacement of an object in a single direction with a constant speed and time over a certain distance. In physics, uniform rectilinear motion can be observed within a plane, on a coordinate axis, where the motion is in a straight line moving in one direction. It is uniform because the speed and time variables on the line are constant.
